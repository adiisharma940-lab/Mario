上弦の鬼 — Identity

Who Is
Akaza?

Once a human named Hakuji, Akaza was forged by tragedy into one of the most powerful demons ever to serve under Muzan Kibutsuji. His body bears the blue tattooed markings of a life defined by suffering and survival.

As the Upper Rank Three of the Twelve Kizuki, Akaza possesses regenerative powers that defy mortal logic — and a combat philosophy that borders on worship. He lives only to find the strongest opponent and defeat them.

Yet beneath the demon's cruelty lies the shattered soul of a boy who once fought to protect everything he loved.

過去 — The Past

The Tragedy
of Hakuji

Childhood — Edo Period

A Boy Born Into Poverty

Hakuji was born to a desperately ill father during Japan's Edo period. With no money for medicine, his father stole to survive — and Hakuji, following in those footsteps, became a thief and brawler himself. He was arrested countless times, bearing new tattoos for every criminal conviction — the very markings that, as a demon, would become his signature.

Adolescence

The Dojo & Keizo

After his father's death, Hakuji was taken in by master Keizo, a sickly dojo owner who recognised the boy's raw, savage talent. Under Keizo's guidance, Hakuji trained in Soryu Art — a powerful water-breathing martial style — and became his adopted son, finding purpose and belonging for the first time.

Love

Koyuki — The Light He Found

Within the dojo lived Koyuki, Keizo's ailing daughter. As Hakuji cared for her, friendship blossomed into love. She promised: "When I get better, let's get married." For the first time, Hakuji had something beyond survival to live for — a future, a home, a reason to be human.

The Breaking Point

Everything Taken Away

Rival dojos, threatened by Hakuji's growing mastery, poisoned both Keizo and Koyuki. He returned home to find them gone. The grief fractured something fundamental in him — he slaughtered the responsible parties with his bare hands, 67 men in one night, fuelled by a grief that had no outlet.

Damnation

Muzan's Transformation

Muzan Kibutsuji found Hakuji in his lowest moment — broken, blood-soaked, with nothing left. He offered power, and Hakuji accepted. The boy named Hakuji ceased to exist. In his place rose Akaza, Upper Moon Three — a demon who would forget his past but carry its pain in every strike, forever searching for a strength great enough to fill the void.
